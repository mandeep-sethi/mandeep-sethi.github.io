Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence, or Emotional Quotient, is defined as one’s ability to identify, understand and handle one’s own emotions as well as perceive what others are feeling. The term Emotional Intelligence was coined by researchers Peter Salavoy and John Mayer, in 1990, and the concept has a purpose in helping us navigate the changeable, uncertain, complex and ambiguous place we live in.


How is it different to IQ?

Intelligence Quotient of a person is derived from its ability to solve problems through a standardised test or a series of tests. Whereas EQ is the ability to identify, evaluate and perceive one’s own and other’s emotions.


Why is it important?

From my personal experience, a required IQ level is not the only capability a company looks for when hiring a candidate, especially if you are applying for leadership positions or higher level jobs. The technical side of things do play a vital part in a tech job but ability to understand the company’s culture and your team’s mindset and emotions is something that would definitely let you excel in your career.
