Learning Plan

1 October 2022

Preparing for bootcamp

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My goal is to gain as much knowledge as possible while at Dev Academy and with this hands-on practical learning start working on free lancing projects along side with a decent job as a software developer. To be able to achieve or atleast reach closest to my goal, I might have to start looking for some internship programmes or if I’m lucky, I’m might land a junior developer role to start off with my career. I also want to make a habit of coding for fun everyday so that it doesn’t become something that I only for money and keeps me motivated.

WWhat do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

An always learning attitude coupled with the ability to stay calm in tough or stressful situations will be my biggest strengths. And my limitations will be sometimes leaning on trying to learn everything and then burning myself out. So the plan is to stick with just-in-time approach and not just-in-case.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I believe pair programming might be a challenge as I’m part of the online cohort and personally it feels ok to have a conversation in person and work on a project, however, pairing up over a zoom/discord call isn’t something I have tried. I feel I’ll get use to it once started.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to work on my fixed mindset traits and evolve into a growth mindset as this will not only help me with the Bootcamp but through out my life both personally and professionally.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect the Bootcamp team to be knowledgeable and motivated mentors who have genuine interest in shaping our career. An approachable and friendly mentor is someone that you would expect in your learning journey.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I’ll stick to my problem-solving techniques and will go through them before reaching out for help. I’ll make sure I have understood a concept before moving on to the next one and will ensure I feel confident about it. I’ll ask for help from peers and mentors whenever I feel lost or have exhausted all other options. This helps in completing the task in a timely manner and burning your self out.

My Bootcamp commitments

I have my desk setup that includes a second screen + mouse + keyboard that should help me speed up my work as I don’t have to switch between different tabs, etc and can use two screens separately i.e. watch and practice at the same time. I’ll be respectful to my mentors, peers and others industry/community representatives and will take an open-mined approach when working in pairs and respect other’s ideas.

Foundations Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I have been working in the tech industry for almost 5 years now juggling different roles under the hat. I have always wanted to contribute to something by actually building it or a part of it, so I have decided to follow my passion and pursue a career in software development. There are a lot of pros of working in the tech industry which I’m living, however, the best part is that you can work from anywhere i.e. flexibility, which I love the most.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning:

My strengths are that I’ve an always learning attitude, and a result it will keep me motivated on my career pathway of becoming a software developer. One of my limitations is that, I sometimes doubt my abilities to perform a task. This is something that I have been working on by taking up on difficult tasks and giving them a shot.

What human skills would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to strengthen my learning mindset and would like to boost my confidence in my skills and expertise. Also, I would like to encourage myself in reaching out to the wider community when struggling to find something as that would help me get to the solution or path to it efficiently.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

One thing that I do and works best for me is planning the whole week in advance and set daily goals. It does take a little bit time of your rest day, however, I have found it extremely useful. There will times where you couldn’t reach your goal but you’ll definitely find a way to do it the next day. Also, using the time box technique with regular breaks in between each task really helps you achieve your results efficiently.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

As a student, I would expect my facilitator to be friendly, knowledgeable and always there to support me in this journey. I would also expect the facilitation team to provide constructive feedback through out the course as this helps one understand what they are doing good and where’s the room for improvement.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

I’m fully committed to the course and I have complete support from my partner.
