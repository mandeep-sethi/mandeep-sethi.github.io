Identity, Values and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

After graduating from India, I made an ethical decision of moving to New Zealand to progress in my life professionally and personally. That was a huge move as I never moved out of house and staying with my family is one of the values that I have. If I look at myself 6 years back, I’m a completely different person and never regret making that move. I have gone so far and I’m constantly pushing myself for more in this beautiful place, Aotearoa, that I call home now.


Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I’m a spiritual person and my culture and religious beliefs have made me discover my spirituality as one of the values. From my culture and people, I have learned a mix of simplicity and curiosity.


Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I believe in continuous learning as it can shape your mindset in anyway you’d like. I’ve taken other courses and I have definitely learned one common pattern from them is that never give up and keep praciticing until you feel 100% confident.


Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I find myself struggling if I’m not sure what I’m doing i.e. if the course is unstructured or I’m spending too much time on a topic which I shouldn’t. After going through some exercises and core material of sprint-1, I have found the material well structured and have followed the JIT approach which has definitely started to show some results.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I can recall my university project where we were a groupd of four working on our final year project. After getting some feedback from our mentor about an improvement, we all had some tension as it was something that was only agreed upon by two of us. I had to take initiative and come up with a mutual idea that had all of us in and was accepted by the mentor as well. It was understanding the dislikes of everyone and getting everyone to respect others views and opinions and taking one step at a time. I’ll be using the same approach of respecting everyone’s views so that everyone feels being heard. This increases productivity as I have experienced it myself in the past. Break down tasks into smaller chunks, assigning it to the team and tackle them one by one helps alot to come up with a better solution and everyone feels inclusive and a part of the project.
