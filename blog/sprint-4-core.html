Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

21 September 2022

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity - meaning our brains are pliable and plastic - refers to how the nervous system changes in response to experience because the nervous system isn’t set in stone and it's constantly changing. Every time we learn something new, we are utilising the power of neuroplasticity. When we learn a new skill, recall a memory or think about something differently, we begin to make new connections between neurons and repeating an activity i.e. practising makes those related connections stronger over time, thus easier for our brain to follow that pathway. If we keep using this pathway, our brains begin to adapt and this new way of thinking, feeling or doing becomes second nature.

Practice makes permanent

Once you realise that your brain can be re-wired and is malleable, it opens up endless opportunities in your life with the fact that anything is possible. When learning to code or learning any skill with neuroplasticity, we can work on improving our focus, generate meaningful connections to the skill we already have, reflect and challenge. This will strengthen your neural pathways and neuroplasticity, hence maximising your knowledge retainment. \ Another good way to form new habits is to tag a new habit onto the end of an existing habit or action you already do (if you want to know more about this, check out BJ Fogg’s book ‘Tiny Habits’).

Growth Mindset

Individuals with a growth mindset believe their talents or skills can be developed through practice, efforts and perseverance. They have a firm belief that everything is really possible if you put your mind to it.

When we commit mistakes, we criticise them as failures and feel upset, however, adopting a growth mindset allows you to recognise these mistakes as a learning opportunity and the mistake will be a lesson well learned. To learn from mistakes, we can have a small retrospection on what went wrong and how it can be improved in the future, all while not being hard on ourselves for making a mistake. All this is a part of learning and growing, where challenges should be treated as opportunities.

Having a growth mindset also motivates you to grow professionally as well as personally. It helps you keep a healthy relationship with your family as you are putting more effort into it, as you would do to learn a new skill.

Something that surprised me in the exploration was the notion that how a growth mindset motivates you to put more effort into your learning. Even if it’s something that you haven’t tried and it’s way too advanced for you, having a growth mindset will tell you to at least give it a go with what skills you have and will push you to learn something new that would help you take on this challenge.

I will adopt a growth mindset in my learning journey by reminding myself that it is okay to not know something, follow the “just-in-case” approach, and start using the word “yet” more often and anything can be learned, and it is within my capabilities to do so.
